Farmers are central figures in the cultivation of crops, a key element of agriculture that ensures the production of essential food and plant-based resources. Their primary role involves managing the entire lifecycle of crops, from planting and nurturing to harvesting and preparing them for market.In today’s world, protecting agricultural crops and ensuring community safety from industrial emissions are crucial. Farmers and residents near factories face unique challenges due to emissions that can impact air and soil quality. Understanding these risks is vital for safeguarding both health and productivity.

About

Agriculture is a vital sector supporting many livelihoods globally. With the rise in industrial development, factories are increasingly being set up near agricultural lands, often without considering their potential impact. It is crucial for landowners and farmers to understand how nearby industrial activities—especially pollutants emitted from factory chimneys—might affect their crops. By utilizing modern technology, they can assess whether these industrial activities are causing harm. This technology is valuable not only for farmers but also for anyone concerned with monitoring and managing environmental pollution in industrial areas.
Agriculture and community health are deeply intertwined with environmental factors, especially around industrial sites. Farmers need to ensure that their crops are not adversely affected by emissions, while local residents require safety from potential pollution.
